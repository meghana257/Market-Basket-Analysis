Project Objective

This project analyzes global online retail transactions to uncover customer purchasing patterns, identify cross-selling opportunities, and generate data-driven recommendations using Market Basket Analysis (MBA). The analysis focuses on transforming raw transactional data into actionable business insights through cleaning, feature engineering, exploratory analysis, and association rule mining.

Dataset Overview

The source is the UCI Online Retail dataset — a real-world transnational transaction log from a UK-based non-store retailer operating between December 2010 and December 2011.

Descriptive Analytics

Retail Analytics Insights

The United Kingdom dominates the retail dataset, contributing the highest number of transactions and generating the majority of total revenue, indicating it as the primary market for customer behavior and sales analysis.

Revenue contribution is highly concentrated, with a significant gap between the UK and other countries, suggesting strong regional dependency and potential opportunities for market expansion in underperforming regions.

Basket size distribution shows that most customers purchase medium-to-large item combinations per transaction, supporting the effectiveness of Market Basket Analysis for identifying product associations and cross-selling opportunities.

Rule Strength Framework

Single Recommendation 1 product triggers 1 recommendation — direct, high-support co-purchase signals

These rules identify the strongest one-to-one co-purchase relationships. When a customer adds a product to their basket, the recommended product has been independently purchased alongside it in a significant share of all UK transactions.

Product PurchasedRecommended ProductSupportConfidenceLift

The Jumbo Bag Red Retrospot acts as the dominant cross-sell anchor across the bag category — five different bag variants consistently lead customers toward it, indicating it is the most universally preferred style in the product family.

With support values between 1.6%–2.8%, these rules appear consistently across UK transactions — making them reliable triggers for automatic "Add this too" recommendation placements at the product and cart level.

Cross-Sell Recommendations 1 product triggers 2 recommendations — extremely high-lift complementary sets

These rules surface the most powerful multi-product cross-sell signals in the dataset. When a customer purchases any single herb marker, they almost certainly also purchase two additional herb markers in the same order — with lift values exceeding 72, these are the strongest behavioral patterns in the entire rule set.

Business Recommendations

Six data-driven recommendations derived directly from the UK retail analysis, association-rule mining, seasonal demand patterns, and customer purchasing behavior uncovered throughout this project.

01
UK Market Priority

Concentrate personalization and retention investment in the UK

The UK accounts for 17,906 of 19,781 global transactions and £8.75M of £10.28M total revenue — 85% market concentration. Personalized loyalty programs, targeted email campaigns, and UK-specific promotions will yield the highest return on marketing spend.

02
Seasonal Stocking

Front-load inventory for the Q4 demand surge from September

Monthly demand data shows November peak demand (527,347 units) represents a 93% uplift over the lowest month. Replenishment cycles for high-volume SKUs — especially the top-10 quantity products — must be calibrated to begin by September to avoid stockouts during the peak window.

03
Peak Hour Targeting

Schedule promotions and campaigns between 10:00 and 14:00

Hourly transaction data shows 11:00–12:00 is the peak purchasing window, with 2,490 transactions — over 3× the daily average hourly rate. Flash sales, email send times, and staffed chat support should be concentrated within the 10:00–14:00 band to maximise conversion during natural demand peaks.

04
Herb Garden Cross-Sell

Deploy a "Complete Your Herb Garden" widget on all herb marker pages

Herb Marker association rules show lift values of 72–76 with confidence up to 0.87 — the strongest signals in the entire dataset. Any customer viewing a single herb marker is an 87% probability buyer of two additional markers. A "Complete the Set" widget on every herb marker product page is the single highest-ROI recommendation action available.

05
Bag Family Bundling

Create a Jumbo Bag collection page and multi-buy incentive

Five bag variants — Jumbo Bag Red Retrospot, Jumbo Bag Apples, Jumbo Bag Pink Polkadot, Lunch Bag Red Retrospot, and Suki Shoulder Bag — are consistently co-purchased with support of 1.7%–2.8%. A curated "Bag Collection" landing page with a 3-for-2 or bundle-discount offer directly leverages this co-purchase signal to increase average order value.

06
Basket Size Growth

Trigger "add one more" prompts at basket sizes of 20+ items

The average UK basket size is 26.44 unique products per transaction, with the largest segment in the 21–30 range. Implementing a dynamic cart prompt — "You're nearly at your average order size — here's what goes well with your basket" — powered by association rules when a basket reaches 20 items will systematically push customers toward and beyond the behavioral average.
